About Me

I am an Algorithm Engineer at Alibaba in Hangzhou, China. I hold Master of Science in Business Analytics at Brandeis University, Bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics and a minor in Linguistics from University of California - San Diego. I have internship experience as a data analyst, product team lead, natural language processing researcher, and marketing analyst. I have a strong quantitative background in data analytics, machine learning and natural language processing; programming skills in Java, Python, R, SQL, MatLab and Tableau; multilingual fluency in English, Chinese, Japanese and Korean.


My Odyssey

I majored in linguistics when I started college to pursue my passion for languages and etymology. As I dived deeper in the field of linguistics, I realized that the way people solve the problems of languages is to apply data science to linguistics. By designing Natural Language Processing algorithms, machines can serve people with insights of human languages.

To become more proficient in data science, I transferred my major to Mathematics. I still kept linguistics as my minor for my passion for linguistics, and became a research assistant at the Experimental Syntax Lab at UCSD for a half year doing the statistics work for linguistics studies. For my interest in Computational Linguistics, I graduated earlier and became a full-time Natural Language Processing intern at Lenovo Research AI Lab training chatbots. During my graduate school, I also interned as a data analyst and product team lead for the start-up company Pakira, building the dataset of companies in wood industry in North America. For more stories of my work, please read them on "Stories" page.

After internship experience, I found that a position between technology and people is a right fit for me. My greatest strength is that I can combine my quantitative side and my people side together. Therefore, I started my career as an algorithm engineer at Alibaba who connects technology with people, to provide companies with data-driven insights. If you are curious about what I did, please check out my "Experience" and "Projects" pages, and have a look at my Github: charliezcr
