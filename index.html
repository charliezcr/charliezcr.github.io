My Odyssey

I majored in linguistics when I entered college in order to pursue my love for languages and etymology. As I dived deeper in the field of linguistics, I realized that data science is a powerful tool for studying the science of languages. It is also the way that people analyze the problems and obtain insights in this age of big data.

To become more proficient in data analytics, I transferred my major to Mathematics and studied lots of linear algebra, optimization, probability, statistics and programming. I still kept linguistics as my minor for my love for linguistics, and became a research assistant job at the Experimental Syntax Lab at UCSD for a half year, doing the statistics work for the Spanish Extraction Study’s data. For my interest in NLP, I graduated earlier in my fourth-year and became a full-time Natural Language Processing intern at Lenovo Research AI Lab, where I trained AI chatbots and reported their improvement. I also collaborated with people from different disciplines, data engineers and non-tech people.

After this internship, I found that a position between tech and non-tech is a right fit for me. I want to combine my quantitative background and understanding of humanity together and start my career as a data analyst who connects technology with people, to make the technology help people make decisions in a humanistic way.
