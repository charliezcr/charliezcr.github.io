My Odyssey

I majored in linguistics when I started my college life in order to pursue my passion for languages and etymology. As I dived deeper in the field of linguistics, I realized that data science is a powerful tool for studying the science of languages. It is also the way that people analyze the problems and obtain insights in this age of big data.

To become more proficient in data analytics, I transferred my major to Mathematics and studied lots of linear algebra, optimization, probability, statistics and programming. I still kept linguistics as my minor for my love for linguistics, and became a research assistant job at the Experimental Syntax Lab at UCSD for a half year, doing the statistics work for linguistics studies. For my interest in NLP, I graduated earlier in my fourth-year and became a full-time Natural Language Processing intern at Lenovo Research AI Lab, where I trained AI chatbots and reported their improvement. For more stories of my work, please read them on Stories page.
